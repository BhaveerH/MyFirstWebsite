Conclusion
The fact that 3 billion people are able to access and contribute to digital platforms can be seen as a positive although most of these contributors/accessors are from countries with a greater internet penetration. “In addition to uneven geographies of access to contemporary modes of communication, uneven geographies of participation and representation are also evident and are in some cases being amplified rather than alleviated”. The information geography giants like North America and Europe should also assist in boosting the infrastructure in places where elements such as politics, broadband affordability and education is lacking. By contributing to these countries, the statistics and spread can be more balanced and allowing greater contribution and access to online platforms to be achieved.
