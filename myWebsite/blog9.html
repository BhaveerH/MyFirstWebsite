The pandemic exposed the essence for a more diverse need of technology through the different sections of society. E-learning was a drastic change that has still not been perfected during the extreme times of the pandemic. Besides e-learning the basic concept of virtual communication, which was existent during the pandemic, has still not affected the digital divide in the manner it should have. At the rate technology is growing with new concepts and inventions taking the world by surprise the world cannot afford for the digital divide to widen.

“One solution has been platforms built for use on basic mobile phones” [1]. It is clearly seen today that there is a vast array of mobile device manufacturers that create devices from an entry-level specification to flagship models. Each year these entry level models aim to provide basic essential features while trying to keep the price at a minimum. These basic features limit the storage space, the RAM, the screen quality, the processor and much more. It can be agreed that mobile devices are capable of being used as a platform for education, but the entry-level models will restrict learners from maximizing their potential and grasping the basic concepts needed.

“But, beside the difficulties of using mobile phones for learning, 40% of students do not have any form of access to the Internet at home” [1]. In addition to the previous paragraph, an entry-level mobile device or in fact any device with internet capabilities is very limited without a connection to the internet. With the rise in data/Wi-Fi prices even the ‘middle-class’ are beginning to feel the strain. Just imagining how areas and societies that are underprivileged could possibly afford a mobile device and data prices is frightening. “The digital divide is complicating educators’ efforts to ensure learning continuity. It is also threatening to exacerbate existing inequalities as the lack of access is disproportionately affecting the most disadvantaged”. It can be seen that just trying to connect to the internet creates a domino effect of problems. From accessibility to an internet capable device to data/Wi-Fi prices etc., there seems to be one problem after another after trying to accomplish one hurdle.

“The UK government is lending laptops to 15 year-old students who did not have one. Egypt has handed free SIM cards to students and has promised to cover the costs of up to a 20% increase in all subscribers' monthly downloads. Thailand has created a scheme of 10 free gigabytes of data usage for mobile users” [1]. Some governments have placed some effort in trying to help society decrease this digital divide, however some countries who do not have the resources and funds struggle to support the people who need it. Those countries who are investing in decreasing the digital divide cannot support those who need it forever. Most of these resources that were allocated only came about due to the pandemic. What will governments do once the pandemic ends? This short-term plan is a temporary solution, but a long-term plan needs to be investigated or else the digital divide will definitely widen.
