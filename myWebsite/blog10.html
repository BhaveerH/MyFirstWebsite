“Information and the access thereto, then, are two sides of the same coin, and few keep the currency” [2]. This statement, abstract yet powerful, perfectly correlates to what has previously been discussed. To be able to access content that can provide inspiration to education and much more that the internet can offer, needs to become the ‘currency’ that a variety of society can keep. Future generations can greatly benefit from this ‘currency’ if and when accessibility and information can be easily available no matter the class of society one is from. “The goal of the institute is to ensure a fair and democratic digital society through a framework of rules and ideas, always guided by human rights. On the internet, as in society and in politics, we all must have a say” [2]. This statement perfectly encapsulates that not only should all societies have access to the internet and its content but also to contribute to it, their own perspectives, and opinions. This contribution could help others find their own voice, transform their life, and give their views a voice.
