After being exposed to Marshal McLuhan’s, “The Medium is the Message”, thinking more deeply about how the medium used could benefit and attract viewers was important. To implement this an abstract background was used. The abstract background provides additional information that I am a person who promotes young, fresh, and risk-taking designs and not afraid to explore abstract ideas. This will communicate to the viewer that I am a web designer who will ensure innovation and creativity in my work.
Communication is a bidirectional element. Learning about how the communication between machine and humans exist was not something previously explored until now. Humans communicate to machines via input such as mouse and keyboard, however, for machines to communicate back many parameters must be implemented for this to be possible. The theory gained about different elements such as semantic markup, colour schemes, tone, metadata tagging and microformats were all executed into the creative and technical aspects. All these individual elements were used in the webpage and work together to help the communication between machine and human to exist and to grow with time.
Learning about internet geographies allowed for a much deeper thought about who around the world gets to view the content uploaded to the web. Not knowing if the content one uploads will actually be viewed by the anticipated viewer becomes a worry towards the content creator. Learning how content is limited and received differently in some countries opens the mind to the endless possibilities of content that we are being withheld against just because one lives in a minority country. Not knowing if one’s content is ‘relevant’ or important enough to be broadcasted throughout the world can discourage people to feel the need to express themselves through their content. However, still knowing that some content may be viewed and can have a positive effect on the viewers, allowing me to have hope and feel the need to upload more content onto the internet.
Throughout the semester it has been learnt that user experience and the user interface rank highly when designing a website. Consistent navigation and general convention make it easier and more natural for a viewer to interact with the site and therefore the browser. Subtle additions to the site to help the viewer’s experience such as gestures of ‘swipe’ or ‘tap’ and how just a simple mouse hover changes the emphasis or colour of a word, allows an animation or slideshow to pause or how they can select an image by just clicking a ‘dot’ makes the viewer to feel in control of the site and unknowingly be communicating with the browser.
