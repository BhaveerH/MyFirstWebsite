The elements of User Experience and User Interface plays an important role in how the machine communicates with the user. Communication includes both the experience and interactions a user is exposed to and affiliated with. Both these elements give a website life by fusing the fundamental foundations of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This critical analysis will discuss the User Experience and User Interface decisions made during the development of my website.

Readability and legibility are an important consideration with regard to UX and UI. “Readability refers to the way in which blocks of texts or large complex chunks of information are arranged on a page. Legibility generally refers to the how the typeface/font is designed” [1]. The font style used was Khand with a San-serif classification. “Generally, San Serif are seen as good for headings and small portions of text” [1]. Minimalistic, crisp, fresh, and modern were the styles achieved and desired by this type of typography.

Another important category of UX design is using tabs. By using tabs, the user can explore the website more without having to use the ‘back’ button built into browsers. Many of the hyperlinks within the webpages opened in a new tab. For most of these hyperlinks, these worked well. However, some of the hyperlinks opening in a new tab were considered to be unnecessary and frustrating to the user. These hyperlinks will be changed in the next iteration to help improve the user experience. The approach will be that links that contain content referring to the current webpage will open in the same tab. Links that refer to external content will be loaded in a new tab.

“SHAPES IMPLY MEANING” [1]. Although in this reading/lecture the shapes are directed specifically towards buttons, the essence could also be extracted for shapes as borders in general. Who would have thought that the simplicity of a curved edge compared to a sharp edge can help dictate formality and tones of a webpage! In my webpages both these types of borders are used to communicate the formality/informality to the user. The aim of using both these styles was to create a balance between professionalism and friendliness.

Responsive design is a necessity in today’s society. The vast topic of accessibly contributes to this, with many users connecting to the web on different devices with different dimensions. This element of development focused only one type of device. Future iterations will definitely incorporate a wider range of responsive design. To be able to reach a wider user base and create a more efficient experience and interface for the user, this will definitely increase the accessibly and communication between the machine (the browser) and the user.

Colours may seem to be a simple element; however, they can define emotions and tones that the user feels. The tones of the colours themselves can affect the user. Too bright and the site becomes difficult to read and to focus on, yet too dull or soft can emote a lack of emotion or passion from the user. “Colour to distinguish between elements in a series of similar forms” [1]. Colour contributes to emphasis and dominance. The colours used in my webpages are of tones that are subtle yet still evoke various emotions of trust, boldness, happiness, and passion.
