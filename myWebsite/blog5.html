Broadband affordability
The cost of broadband is one that would directly affect the number of people contributing and accessing the internet. A trend noticed was that the internet penetration per country was directly proportional to the broadband affordability. Anomalies included India and Sri Lanka which with growth within the last few years start to match the trend shown. Where the trend shows that internet penetration is difficult to nearly impossible to obtain with the broadband completely unaffordable to the local people will not afford them the opportunity to access and contribute to the digital world. “It is important to remember that there are now almost 3 billion internet users who do have some form of access”. In the time that the digital world was created to give people the opportunity to share, contribute and access is still a great feat, however, there is always opportunity for this number to grow but it is significantly slowed by the element of cost of broadband affordability.

Geographies of participation
“There appears to be large differences between access to information and participating in its creation and distribution”. Only content access seemed to be a better spread across the world compared to content production. Content production was more prioritized in certain parts of Europe and North America. It can be anticipated that as countries experience a more stable growth to internet penetration that countries will start to gain confidence in content creation. The countries that contributed more at this time were ones that would have been able to provide exposure to its people about how contribution is done as well as more relaxed political pressure.
GitHub and Wikipedia are some of the largest platforms to share information and content around the world. “In this specific instance, we were only able to use data about the English version of Wikipedia because the method of geolocating user profiles involved parsing unstructured English-language text”. This could prove to be an under-estimation of the countries whose first language is not English and an over-estimation of the English-speaking countries. As for GitHub commits, I believe that would fall under the education standards of the countries in question. The quality of education in certain African countries are not up to the standard of European, American and some Asian countries. GitHub commits rely heavily on users uploading code, basic education in technology can greatly increase the growth of participation on large digital platforms like these.

Geographies of representation
Once again it is clearly demonstrated throughout the text and graphical representations that South America, Middle East, and Africa are the places that are the poorest in terms of graphical representations. In terms of Geonames, some of the data can be considered skewed as certain places had projects funded by the European Union to create a geographical information infrastructure. This can be considered a good thing as it allows for a greater representation to be obtained by that country. However, as countries continue their steady growth the data will become less skewed and balanced out.
