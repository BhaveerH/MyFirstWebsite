Header and Footer
The header and footer used in the webpages contain a grey background that is partially blurred to provide the desired effect of frosted glass. This was used to make the content placed on them more prominent. The header contained important information such as the title/author’s name as well as a navigation panel to the other webpages. The navigation panel is placed in the header for easy access and optimization. The footer contained a panel with the programming languages I am proficient in. This was included in the footer to provide easy access to this additional but important information. Throughout the webpages, the style of the header and footer showed consistency so that the viewer does not become overwhelmed by the number of changes.

Overall Design
The overall design of the webpages was divided into two or three sections. This was to keep the viewer engaged within the webpages and to not overwhelm the viewer with too much information at once. The homepage consisted of three main sections. The first section was very simple and minimalist but grabbed the viewer’s attention with the large, bold font and colour. This section would fill the centre of the screen and would be important to catch the viewer’s attention and captivate them to explore more of the webpages. The second section contains more information about who the website is about and an image of them. The third section would allow them to explore more due to the simple and aesthetic nature of the navigation system. The other webpages contained two sections. The first section displayed to the viewer a contents list to other webpages that they may find interest in or provide them with personal information. The second section expands on the link chosen by the viewer in the first section. By using a contents list the viewer is helped quickly to locate a topic that interests them. This allows for the viewer to not scroll through long and tedious webpages to find what they are looking for. This method provided a more efficient way of keeping the viewer engaged and informed in an easily accessible way.
